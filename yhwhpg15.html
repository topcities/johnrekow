By the way, the Magi are known to be Magian, originally Median, who seem to have conformed to the Persian religion, whilst retaining some of their old beliefs.
They are thought to be Oriental scientists, magicians or wizards.
These Oriental wise men could have been teachers, priests, physicians, astrologers, seers, interpreters of dreams, augers, soothsayers or sorcerers.
The Bible does not tell us how many of them there were.
Some people think that there were three wise men, but the Bible does not tell us how many of them were actually there.

The next song is technically a Christmas carol.
Yet, it is interesting to read this at any time throughout the year,
because it reminds us of who the Saviour is, and it does have some interesting lyrics
that praise Him, and remind us of the work that He did for us so that we can be forgiven and saved
with His free gift of Eternal Life.


O Holy Night (O Night Divine) is a well-known Christmas carol composed by Adolphe Adam in 1847 to the French poem which would be titled, in English, as, Midnight, Christians, was written by wine merchant and poet Placide Cappeau (1808–1877).

The poem and song came from the city of
-- In French, it is pronounced, Rook Moo-ur --
Roquemaure, in southern France.

The song was premiered in the city of Roquemaure in 1847.
Unitarian minister John Sullivan Dwight,
editor of Dwight's Journal of Music,
translated the song into English lyrics in 1855.

The book of Ecclesiasticus Sirach is a Jewish work originally in Hebrew of ethical teachings, from approximately 200 to 175 BC, written by the Jewish scribe Ben Sira of Jerusalem, on the inspiration of his father Joshua son of Sirach, sometimes called Jesus son of Sirach or Yeshua ben Eliezer ben Sira.
In Egypt, it was translated into Greek by the author's unnamed grandson, who added a prologue.
