But you will need to put your feelings aside for a time so you can help your child feel safe, so that he or she will be able to tell you about what has happened.

Listen carefully to your child without interrupting. Let your child tell the story in their own words and at their own pace.
Believe your child. Tell your child that you believe them, even if what your child is saying seems unreal or doesn't make sense at first.
Tell your child that it's not their fault, they're not to blame, and they won't be punished.
Tell your child they've done the right thing by telling you. Your child has been brave to tell you about the abuse, so acknowledge your child's courage.
Talk with your child about how you can help them feel safe and loved. Be prepared to do what your child needs you to do.

Your child might not tell you everything at once. Your child might tell you more about it gradually over several days or weeks. Be patient and give your child time to share experiences and feelings.

It's also important to be available in case your child wants to share more or needs you to be around to feel safe. This could mean staying at home with your child, taking time off work, picking your child up from school, and checking in with your child regularly.

Your child might ask you to promise not to tell anyone else. You can't make that promise. But you can say that you'll do your best to keep your child safe. You can say that keeping your child safe might mean you'll need to talk to other people, but you need to let your child know what you are going to do.
It's a good idea to make notes about what has happened so that you can give accurate details to the police.

Child sexual abuse is always a crime.
If you think that your child or the child you're caring for has been abused or if you think there's an immediate risk of abuse, you should report it.
If you think a child has been abused, tell the police.
In the UK, phone 999 -- In Australia, phone 000 -- In the USA, call 911.

Read your Bible, every day, even just a little bit every day if you can, study it, and try your best to do what the Bible says, and obey it.

What does the word, confound, mean?
Referring again to the Longman, Chambers, Cambridge, Collins and Oxford Dictionaries,
in January 2022, we went to those online Dictionaries to look at, combine and merge together their word definitions
for the word, confound.
confound, means,
to confuse and surprise people by being unexpected
to prove someone or something wrong
to defeat an enemy, plan etc
to puzzle; to baffle.
to defeat or thwart enemies or their schemes.
to mix up or confuse one thing with another.
to confuse and very much surprise someone, so that they are unable to explain or deal with a situation.
If someone or something confounds you, they make you feel surprised or confused, often by showing you that your opinions or expectations of them were wrong.
to astound or perplex; bewilder
to mix up; confuse

Commentary: How do you live forever?
How do you get eternal life?

Not by bread alone, but you live by every word that proceeded out of the mouth of God.
What does that mean? It sounds like a riddle! Here is an idea to consider. Maybe this is the answer to the riddle.
The bread is the bread of life, which is Jesus, Yaw-Who-Shaw. To receive and eat that bread would be to believe and receive the New Testament, the new Covenant, the bread, broken for you.

Every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God, that would be when God spoke and gave us the commandments in the Old Testament, the Torah, and Moses wrote them down. Look closely at the Hebrew language, and you will see that The Ten Commandments are also known as the Ten Words. It says, every word, that would be each and every commandment, as in, all of the commandments, not some, not half, not most, but all commandments. Obey every commandment, every single word of them.

Put these two together, the bread of life, and every word came out of the mouth of God, and live.
The living bread of life points to the New Testament, and the word of God, the commandments of EE-Low-Heem, that points to the Old Testament, the Old Covenant.

Your relationship with the Heavenly Father, Yaw-Who-Wah, is like a marriage relationship, not of the human, physical, gender kind, but of a spiritual kind.

The New Testament is getting married, and the Old Testament tells you how to stay successfully married.

If you only read and obey the Old Testament and you ignore the New Testament, that is like doing the boyfriend-girlfriend dating relationship, but you are not technically or legally married. You act like you are, you pretend like you are, but you're not. You can pretend, and do sexual activity like a married couple, and share your time, your decisions, and your finances, but you are still not married. It's like someone who rejects Jesus, Yaw-Who-Shaw, and all they do is try to obey the commandments in the Old Testament Torah. Doing all of that, without Jesus, Yaw-Who-Shaw, will not get you into heaven. It's also like perfectly obeying all of the traffic laws, but never getting a legal licence to drive.


Other people like to read and obey the New Testament and ignore the Old Testament. That is like actually getting married, and then staying exactly the same, and living your life like you never got married, even though you point to the marriage license, John 3:16 and Romans 10:9, telling everyone you are married, but you refuse to let the marriage change you in any way. Let's say, as a man, before you were married, you always went out drinking with the guys for hours at night, then you spend your Saturdays, spending hours watching porn and playing video games, then you spend hours on Sunday doing nothing but watching football. Then you get married, and you keep doing that?

Or let's say, as a woman, when you were single, you spent hours doing the most amazing dance moves on the discotheque dance floor at the local nightclub, flirting with every hot guy. Then, you get married, and before you know it, here is your baby crying because its nappy (its diaper) needs changing, but you're too busy dancing and texting all your guy friends? Does that sound like a marriage that's going to last? Believing in Jesus and then staying exactly the same, ignoring all of His commandments? A new heart I will give you? No, thanks. Be transformed by the renewing of you mind? Not now. I'm too busy making my next Tik Tok video of me doing sexy dancing whilst the baby keeps crying? That's like getting your legal licence to drive, but then you continue ignoring all traffic laws in the driver's manual. What? Actually stop at a red traffic light or stop sign? I'm supposed to drive on the right side of the road in the United States? I'm saved and forgiven, I'm under grace, I'm not under the law. If I ignore and forget and disobey all traffic laws in the driver's manual, the Torah, the Bible, how soon will I crash and die?

Getting a driving licence is one thing, but continuing to obey the traffic laws in the driver's manual, is what makes your driving successful.

Getting married is one thing, but staying married is what makes a marriage be successful. Getting married is New Testament believing in and receiving the Messiah, the Muh-She-Awk, and staying married, is Old Testament, Torah Commandment Obedience. Those verses that we just looked at, they tell us that He lives in you, dwells in you, and causes you to obey his commandments, by fixing your heart and transforming your mind.

Since the first shall be last and the last shall be first, eat the bread, then eat the scroll and live. Receive the New Testament, and obey the Old Testament, and live. Receive the bread of life, and obey every word that proceeded out of the mouth of Elohim, and live, forever. Receive the Messiah and obey the Torah, and live, forever. You can't live by bread alone. Get both, read, study, pray, receive, believe, follow, trust, obey, forgive, and live.
