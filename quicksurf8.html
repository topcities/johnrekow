Natural Ways to Repel Pests

FYI:  Natural ways to repel pests:

Flies hate the smell of Cloves, Basil, Eucalyptus, Lavender and Vinegar.

Fleas hate the smell of Garlic, Lemons and Limes, Borax, Lavender, Eucalyptus and Vinegar.

Mice hate the smell of Peppermint and Mothballs.

Cats hate the smell of orange, lemon, lime, grapefruit, lavender, geranium, eucalyptus, rosemary, thyme, and rue.

Moths hate the smell of Cedar, Lavender, sandalwood, eucalyptus, geranium, cinnamon, orange blossom, lemongrass, peppermint, bergamot, bay leaves, cloves, rosemary, mint, thyme, patchouli or combinations of them.
(Patchouli is a flowering plant from Southeast Asia, used for making herbal tea, perfume, insect repellent, and incense.)

Bedbugs hate the smell of Diatomaceous earth, Alcohol, Lavender, Cinnamon, mint, garlic, Cayenne Pepper, Tea tree oil, Lemon juice, and Blood Orange oil.


Earwig trap: Get a used, low-sided can (like the size of a small tuna can) and fill it with 1/2 inch of used vegetable oil.
Leave the can where earwigs have been observed but where it cannot be accidentally kicked over.
You can even put a slice of apple inside to make the trap look more attractive to earwigs.
Leave overnight, and the next day, you will see that the earwigs have made their way into the can and drowned.

Based on the style and formatting advice and examples at the above University of Oxford website,
the following format is good for a covering letter.
This is not an absolute standard. Covering letters are allowed to vary slightly in their format and structure.


Top of covering letter page:
Your address, aligned RIGHT (your name is NOT up here)
Empty line.
Employer's name and address -- an exact person's name is greatly preferred, aligned LEFT.
On the immediate next line, today's date, aligned RIGHT
Empty line.
Empty line.
Dear Mr Name (no comma after surname)
Empty line.
Paragraph 1.
Empty line.
Paragraph 2.
Empty line.
Paragraph 3.
Empty line.
Empty line.
Yours sincerely (no comma or punctuation)
Empty line.
Your Signature
Empty line.
Your first and surname, printed out.

Here is some advice about what the above three paragraphs should be about:

First paragraph:
INTRODUCTION

Introduce yourself.
Explain why you are writing.
If you are responding to an advert, say where you saw it.


Second paragraph, or two short paragraphs of each if the need arises.
WHY THIS JOB?

Explain why you're interested in the job and the organisation.
Target the letter at the organisation. Check the company's website and CareerConnect for company information. Make it implicit that you have not simply sent out multiple copies of the same letter to different employers.
If you can, say something original about the organisation.
Explain what factors attract you to them – don't just repeat text from their publicity material.


WHY ME?
Use the remainder to explain why you are well-suited to the position.
Refer to relevant skills, experience and knowledge that you have.
Match what you say to the requirements given in the job advertisement.
If a job specification has "essential" criteria, your letter may become lengthier than otherwise – as you should indicate how you meet each criterion.
Try to avoid using exactly the same phrases which appear on your CV.

Third or last paragraph:
CONCLUSION
Reiterate your desire to join the organisation.
Add a 'look forward to hearing from you'-type statement.




TOP TIPS FOR A SUCCESSFUL COVERING LETTER
Don't exceed one page. An exception is if a person specification lists criteria applicants must meet. In this case, try not to exceed two pages.
Write to a named person if you possibly can – rather than Dear Sir/Madam.
Check your spelling and get someone else to read it over.
Don’t start every sentence with "I".
Give evidence for all your claims.
Be enthusiastic!
Don’t repeat your whole CV.
End "Yours sincerely", and type your name and title – don't forget to sign the letter if you're printing it out!


The next bit of advice below comes from the following website:

RFUT:  University of Kent Careers:  How To Write a Successful Covering Letter
http://www.kent.ac.uk/careers/cv/coveringletters.htm

Most employers want candidates to submit a cover letter for each position.
Most employers preferred half a page, some felt the shorter the better!
It should never be longer than one page long.
If emailed put your covering letter in the body of the email. If you attach it with nothing in the email body it may be misidentified as spam.
Use your own words, not formal long-winded clichés.
Action verbs can help to make it sound better.
Answer the question "Why should I see you?"
Make the person who reads it feel special: that it is addressed to them personally and not one of fifty identical letters you are sending out without thought or care,
You might include your understanding of the work/knowledge of the company,
You must say why you decided to pursue this career.
Say when you're available to start work.
