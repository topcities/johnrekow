When should we do the Passover? There is an argument over how to figure out when to do the Passover. Some look at a general calendar that predicts when it should be done. Some look at a barley harvest, and decide when to do the Passover based on the stage of the barley's ripeness and maturity at a certain time of year. Others look at the moon and stars. They observe a specific phase of the moon at a certain time in the spring, and count the days from that observation to decide when to do the Passover. Others look at the constellations in the stars of the sky, and when certain groups of stars are at a certain angle or position in the sky, this observation is used to calculate when to do the Passover. There is an argument over which combination of these methods is the right way to determine when to do the Passover. As a result, some do the Passover a month sooner, and some do this feast a month later.

9 When I wrote to you before, I told you not to associate with people who indulge in sexual sin.
10 But I wasn't talking about unbelievers who indulge in sexual sin, or are greedy, or cheat people, or worship idols. You would have to leave this world to avoid people like that.
(I meant that you are not to associate with anyone who claims to be a brother)
11 I meant that you are not to associate with anyone who claims to be a believer yet indulges in sexual sin, or is greedy, or worships idols, or is abusive, or is a drunkard, or cheats people. Don't even eat with such people.
12 It isn't my responsibility to judge outsiders, but it certainly is your responsibility to judge those inside the church who are sinning.
13 God will judge those on the outside; but as the Scriptures say (in Deuteronomy 17:7), "You must remove the evil person from amongst you."

Numbers 33: 3 & 4 NLT
