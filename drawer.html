Pizza Dough -- Version 1.0 -- August 2022


Pizza Dough

These are all USA units of measurement.

Reminder:
Make sure all meats were cooked hot enough or long enough to be safe to eat.
You could pre-cook the raw meats separately beforehand to ensure that they have reached a safe internal temperature.

Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.

The crusts will be spread out onto a cookie sheet.
Use parchment paper, or else slightly oil or grease the cookie sheet to keep the crust from sticking.

Ingredients:
2 cups of Almond Flour
2/3 of a cup of Arrowroot
1/4 cup of Flax Meal
1 Tablespoon of cauliflower powder
A Half Tablespoon of Baking Soda
1/4 cup of Shredded Parmesan Cheese
1/2 teaspoon of Salt
One and a half teaspoons of Organic Italian Seasoning
A Half teaspoon of garlic powder -- or granulated garlic
A pinch of black pepper -- or use more if you like black pepper -- add to taste
Two full Tablespoons of Apple Cider Vinegar
1/4 cup of Oil (cooking oil) -- Avocado Oil is most recommended
1/3 cup of filtered water (avoid tap water for best taste)
1/2 teaspoon of Cream of Tartar

Have a tablespoon standing by in case you need to add more water.

Put on disposable gloves.
Lightly oil your hands if not wearing gloves.
Use your hands to mix well all ingredients together.

If the dough seems too dry or crumbly, add two more full Tablespoons of water.

This amount can be pressed into two eight-inch crusts, or press it into one square, 10 inches by 10 inches.

Do not add any toppings yet.

With the oven now at 350 degrees Fahrenheit,
Bake the crust for 15 minutes, without any toppings on it.
Bake it until the crust looks just slightly golden brown.

Take it out, but keep the oven on.
Now add your preferred pizza sauce and toppings.

Put it back in and bake it with all the sauce and toppings on it,
for about 15 to 20 minutes more.

Serve and enjoy!

Start by making the almond flour pie crust
Gluten-free, sugar-free, and keto.

almond flour pie crust
INGREDIENTS YOU NEED:
Two and a half cups -- 2 cups and a 1/2 cup Blanched almond flour
1/3 cup Erythritol (or any sweetener of choice -- or -- leave this out for a savoury pie crust)
1/4 tsp Sea salt (or 1/2 tsp for savoury pie crust)
1/4 cup Ghee (measured solid, then melted)
1 large Egg (or approx. 2 tbsp additional ghee)
OPTIONAL: 1/2 tsp Vanilla extract (optional)

SUGGESTED MODIFICATION: Use 1/4 cup of Butter instead of Ghee
SUGGESTED MODIFICATION: If Salted Butter, then do not add salt
SUGGESTED MODIFICATION: If Unsalted Butter, then add the Sea Salt listed above

More information about the pie crust ingredients, and how to find them, are listed after the following instructions...


almond flour pie crust

1.
Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (177 degrees C). Line the bottom of a 9 in (23 cm) round pie pan with parchment paper, or grease well.
2.
In a large bowl, mix together the almond flour, erythritol (if using), and sea salt.
3.
Stir in the melted ghee and egg, until well combined. (If using vanilla, stir that into the melted ghee before adding to the dry ingredients.) The "dough" will be dry and crumbly. Just keep mixing, pressing and stirring, until it's uniform and there is no almond flour powder left. (Alternatively, you can use a food processor to mix it all together.)
4.
Press the dough into the bottom of the prepared pan. You can flute the edges if desired; if it crumbles when doing this, just press it back together. Carefully poke holes in the surface using a fork to prevent bubbling.
5.
Bake the crust for 10-12 minutes, until lightly golden. (Add fillings only after pre-baking.)

Ghee is clarified butter that originated in ancient India.

Ghee -- how to make Clarified Butter
Clarified butter is milk fat rendered from butter
to separate the milk solids and water from the butterfat.
Typically, it is produced by melting butter and allowing
the components to separate by density. The water evaporates,
some solids float to the surface and are skimmed off,
and the remainder of the milk solids sink to the bottom
and are left behind when the butterfat (which would then be on top)
is poured off. This butterfat is the clarified butter.

Pumpkin Pie Filling:

1.
Beat together all of the Pie Filling ingredients at medium-low speed, until smooth. (Don't overmix.)
2.
When the pie crust is done baking, reduce the oven temperature to 325 degrees F (163 degrees C). Cool the crust on the counter for at least 10 minutes, longer if you have time.
3.
Pour the filling into the crust. Gently tap on the counter to release air bubbles.
4.
Bake for 40-50 minutes, until the pie is almost set but still slightly jiggly in the centre. (Check on it occasionally, and if you see the crust start to brown, cover the crust edge with foil and return to the oven until the filling is done. It should still jiggle a bit in the centre, like a custard before it sets.)
5.
Cool completely on the counter, then refrigerate at least an hour before slicing. Pie can be refrigerated overnight.

Allspice, also called pimento, Jamaica pimento, Jamaica pepper,
pimenta, or myrtle pepper, is the dried unripe fruit
(berries, used as a spice) of Pimenta dioica,
a midcanopy tree native to the Greater Antilles,
southern Mexico, and Central America,
now cultivated in many warm parts of the world.
The name "allspice" was coined as early as 1621 by the English,
who thought it combined the flavour of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
