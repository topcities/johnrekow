Let's begin by remembering a little detail taken from the beginning of the book of Genesis,
about the story of The Man and Woman in Eden...

Genesis 2:17 (KJV)
but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die.

and then, in chapter 5, we see this...

Genesis 5:5 (NLT)
Adam lived 930 years, and then he died.

Nine hundred and thirty years is a bit longer than one day.
Did we just find a contradiction in the Bible?
If you were to read your entire Bible first, you eventually read this next part...



In the New Testament,
Peter writes in his second letter about how The Day of the Lord Is Coming...

2 Peter 3:8 (KJV)
But, beloved, be not ignorant of this one thing, that one day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day.

Peter is quoting from the Book of Jubilees.


The Book of Jubilees is not in the Bible.
The Book of Jubilees is also known as
The Book of Division, or
The Lesser Genesis, or the Little Genesis.


Only about four copies of the Book of Jubilees
were known to exist until 1947.
They were the four complete Ge'ez texts also known as
the Ethiopic Version.
Those four copies were made in the 15th and 16th centuries.
Then, between 1947 and 1956, 15 more copies were found
in the Dead Sea Scrolls, written in Hebrew.
The Ge'ez version, also known as the Ethiopic Version,
is a Hebrew-to-Ethiopic translation written in an Ethiopian Semitic language,
and has been shown to be an accurate translation when compared to
the 15 Hebrew copies found in the Dead Sea Scrolls.

The original Hebrew copies of the Book of Jubilees may have been written
sometime between 160 to 150 BC.


Robert Henry (R. H.) Charles, was born in 1855, and he died in 1931.
He translated The Ethiopic Version of the Hebrew Book of Jubilees, and first published an English translation in 1895.
It was not the first English translation at that time, but this one still seems very popular even today.

Most people even today like to read and quote from the
R.H. (Robert Henry) Charles English Translation (1895, 1902, 1913, 1917)

The book of Jubilees in the Eth Cepher is also based on this translation.

Peter is remembering the Book of Jubilees 4: 29 & 30.

Here is Jubilees 4: 29 & 30 from the
R.H. (Robert Henry) Charles English Translation (1895, 1902, 1913, 1917)

And at the close of the nineteenth jubilee, in the seventh week in the sixth year thereof, Adam died, and all his sons buried him in the land of his creation, and he was the first to be buried in the earth.
And he lacked seventy years of one thousand years; for one thousand years are as one day in the testimony of the heavens and therefore was it written concerning the tree of knowledge: "On the day that ye eat thereof ye will die."
For this reason he did not complete the years of this day; for he died during it.

Commentary:
Adam's death at seventy years short of a thousand is a literal fulfilment of the curse, in Genesis 2:17, because he did die in "the day" in which he ate the forbidden fruit, one day being with the Lord as a thousand years.
In less than one so-called day, or less than 1000 years, Adam died.

Any trained scholar can now look at the Hebrew copies from the Dead Sea Scrolls and make an English translation.
A very similar English translation would be as follows...
And at the end of the nineteenth jubilee in the seventh week, in the sixth year, Adam died.
And all of his children buried him in the land of his creation.
And he was the first who was buried in the earth.
And he lacked seventy years from one thousand years, for a thousand years are like one day in the testimony of heaven and therefore it was written concerning the tree of knowledge, "In the day you eat from it you will die."
Therefore he did not complete the years of this day because he died in it.



Before R.H. Charles, there was
Another popular English translation by
Reverend George Henry Schodde,
Professor at Capital University, in Columbus, Ohio.
This English translation was Published in 1888, which came out seven years earlier than the one by R.H. Charles.

George Henry Schodde was born in 1854, and he died in 1917.
The American, George Henry Schodde, was just a little more than 1 year and 3 months older than the British man, R.H. Charles.


Jubilees 4: 29 & 30
The verse numbering system is off by one in this other translation.
What is verse 29 and 30 in the 1895 R.H. Charles version is verse 28 and 29 in this 1888 George Henry Schodde translation.


Jubilees 4: 28 & 29 -- George Henry Schodde English Translation

28
And at the end of the nineteenth jubilee, in the seventh week, in the sixth year thereof, Adam died, and all of his sons buried him in the land of the creation of Adam, and he was the first to be buried in the earth, and he lacked seventy years of one thousand years; for one thousand years are like one day in the testimony of heaven, and therefore it was written concerning the tree of knowledge: " On the day on which ye eat thereof ye shall die."
29
And for this reason he did not complete the years of this day; for in it he died.

In conclusion, what may at first appear to be a contradiction, will pan out to be actually true, if you read your entire Bible from cover to cover.
One who claims a Bible contradiction is the same one who has never carefully studied the entire Bible.
If you woke up this morning, and you're still not yet in Heaven, then, keep on reading your Bible!
Read your Bible and pray, every single day.
You'll be glad you did, forever.

In February of 2023, these were the dictionary definitions for
discernment and discretion



Longman Dictionary
discernment:
the ability to make good judgements...when you notice or understand something


Chambers Dictionary
discernment:
to perceive, notice or make out something; to judge.
having or showing good judgement.

Cambridge Dictionary
discernment:
the ability to judge people and things well


Collins Dictionary
discernment:
Discernment is the ability to judge which things of a particular kind are good and which are bad.


Oxford Dictionary
discernment:
the ability to show good judgement about the quality of somebody/something




Longman Dictionary
discretion:
the ability and right to decide exactly what should be done in a particular situation,
the ability to deal with situations in a way that does not offend, upset, or embarrass people or tell any of their secrets


Chambers Dictionary
discretion:
the quality of behaving in a discreet way.
the ability to make wise judgements.
the freedom or right to make decisions and do as one thinks best.


Cambridge Dictionary
discretion:
the ability to behave without causing embarrassment or attracting too much attention, especially by keeping information secret
the right or ability to decide something
choice, or the right to make a choice, based on judgement
the right to choose something, or to choose to do something, according to what seems most suitable in a particular situation



Collins Dictionary
discretion:
Discretion is the quality of behaving in a quiet and controlled way without drawing attention to yourself or giving away personal or private information.
If someone in a position of authority uses their discretion or has the discretion to do something in a particular situation, they have the freedom and authority to decide what to do.



Oxford Dictionary
discretion:
the freedom or power to decide what should be done in a particular situation,
care in what you say or do, in order to keep something secret or to avoid causing difficulty for somebody or making them feel embarrassed; the quality of being discreet
