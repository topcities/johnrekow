Mourning




Commentary:

In January 2019, I was mourning the loss of my two old dogs. Because of this, I wanted to look at the Bible verses that talk about mourning. We will look at those Bible verses here in just a moment.

It was just before 10am on the morning of Wednesday, the 30th day of January 2019, when a veterinarian completed the medical procedure that carefully and gently brought the lives of my two old dogs to an end.

The larger dog was Bammy. Bammy looked like a black Labrador Retriever and yet was also half pit bull. She mainly just looked like a black lab, mostly all black with some white down the middle of the front neck, chest and belly, with a touch of white on the end of her paws. She was very calm, peaceful and loving, gentle and careful. You could not even tell that she was half pit, but you might vaguely wonder about it if you carefully studied the shape of her head.

The smaller dog was named, Chopper. Chopper was a tan-brown deer Chihuahua, but he did not behave like one at all. He was never nervous, never growly, never nippy, and never shaky. He only would bark when you would expect a faithful watchdog to bark, and not unnecessarily, unless you wanted him to. He was very calm and easy-going, and would let you carry him around, or even push him about in a pram (a baby buggy). He would just go along with whatever you were doing, and was happy just to be with you and lay by your side or on your lap.

The larger dog, Bammy, was born sometime in February of 2009, and joined our family when she was 9 months old, in November of 2009. She wagged her tail all the time, even whilst eating her food, which I saw as her way of being so thankful, and she loved running with the kids on the playground equipment and going down the slide. She also was always on the job, protecting us, being like it says there in Hebrews 4:12, she had a gift of being able to be a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart. After many years of her gentle companionship and protection as our watchdog, she was getting to be in a great deal of pain, with arthritis, skin diseases and infections. When her life ended, she was 9 years old, and she would have turned 10 years old if she would have lived for just a few more weeks.

The smaller dog, Chopper, was born sometime in April of 2005, and was rescued from an animal shelter. He was adopted into our family on Saturday 14 July 2007, when he was 2 years old. He loved chasing the ball and would even take turns going up to each child at the playground, laying the ball down at their feet, offering each kid an opportunity to throw it for him, and at the end of the day, he would snuggle up and rest his little head on the shoulder of the one who was holding him. We even taught him how to, kind of, talk. I could have sworn I heard him say a few times, words, like, ma ma, and, I, love, you. After many years of his energetic playfulness and love, old age was taking its toll on him. He was losing his eyesight, he was losing his hearing, he had a tender spot on his back, and he was having moments of incontinence. When the time came for him to pass away, he was 13 years old. If he would have lived for just a few more months, he would have turned 14 in April of 2019.

Both of the dogs had lumps and bumps on or under their skin, which were probably tumours of some kind.

Just over a week after that day when we said good-bye to our two old dogs, I typed-up these words here, so that I would not forget the things about them that made me love them both so much. I have a short list here of some of their characteristics.

Love. They demonstrated true, unconditional Love, affection, benevolence and charity. We were well-loved by them, and they loved us, no matter what. They reminded me of Proverbs 17:17 (KJV) where it says, A friend loveth at all times....

Trust. They had put their complete and total trust in us at all times, and in all situations. The larger dog sometimes had nails to be trimmed, and the smaller dog had a little bad tooth to be pulled out, which had to be done on two occasions over the years. They totally trusted us during those times, and they even trusted us all the way to the very final hour and minute of their lives -- they completely trusted us.

Loyalty and faithfulness. They were very loyal and faithful.

Obedience. They were very obedient. The Dog Whisperer, Cesar Millan would have been very proud of them, and the family that they belonged to.

Friendship. They were our friends. They were like a friend that always loved you at all times, and in all seasons, in all places, circumstances and situations.

They comforted us, they gave us comfort, they gave us peace, and they protected us, every day and throughout every night.

I want to be like that for God, I want to be like that for the people in my life, and I want to also be remembered that way after I'm gone.

There will be times and seasons that include moments when one may have mourning, sadness and weeping. We're going to take a look at some Bible verses here that talk about mourning.

Psalms 139:1-3 KJV
1 O Lord, thou hast searched me, and known me.
2 Thou knowest my downsitting and mine uprising, thou understandest my thought afar off.
3 Thou compassest my path and my lying down, and art acquainted with all my ways.
(Thou winnowest my path and my lying down, and art acquainted with all my ways.)
The Hebrew word used there in verse 3 for, compassest, is pronounced, zaw-raw.
The definitions for zaw-raw, according to the Strong's Hebrew Lexicon, and the Brown-Driver-Briggs' Hebrew Definitions, are...
to toss about, to diffuse, winnow
cast away, compass, disperse, fan, scatter (away), spread, strew, winnow, sift.


By the way, this second reference book, titled, A Hebrew and English Lexicon of the Old Testament,
is more commonly known as the Brown–Driver–Briggs or BDB, which is based on the name of its three authors.
The BDB is a standard reference for Biblical Hebrew and Aramaic, first published in the year 1906.


The Longman Dictionary of Contemporary English says that, winnow, means,
to make a list, group, or quantity smaller by getting rid of the things that you do not need or want

The Definition of "winnow" from the Cambridge Advanced Learner's Dictionary:
to blow the chaff (the outer coverings) from grain before it can be used as food.
to reduce a large number of people or things to a much smaller number by judging their quality.


The HarperCollins Cobuild Advanced English Dictionary says,
If you winnow a group of things or people, you reduce its size by separating the ones that are useful or relevant from the ones that are not.

Commentary:
So, if the Lord winnows the path that you are walking on, we could say that the Lord goes before you, and checks the path you are on before you get there, the path in front of you, and clears out the people and things that are down the path before you get there.
I imagine the Lord getting people and things off of my path before I get there;
people and things that could stop or block me, or hinder me, distract me, slow me down, people and things that might make me trip or cause me to walk off of the path that the Lord wants me to walk on;
people and things that might interfere with me going in the direction that the Lord wants me to travel.
He may also keep people and things on my path, on purpose.
The Lord may put things and people into your path, people and things that are on your path to help you;
people, things and situations that are intended for you, and are placed or kept there on or along your path, because those people and things are a part of God's plan and purpose for you and your life.
