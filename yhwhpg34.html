The books of Susanna, Bel and the Dragon, and Additions to Daniel are part of the Apocrypha and are often included in various translations of the Bible.
Here's a comparison and analysis of these three texts:

Susanna:
- Summary: Susanna tells the story of a virtuous Jewish woman falsely accused of adultery by two elders, Daniel intervenes and proves her innocence.
- Key Points: Focuses on the theme of justice, innocence, and the wisdom of Daniel in delivering Susanna from false accusation.

Bel and the Dragon:
- Summary: Bel and the Dragon consists of two separate stories. In one, Daniel exposes the falsehood of idol worship by showing that the idol Bel cannot eat. In the other, Daniel kills a dragon worshipped as a god in Babylonia.
- Key Points: Illustrates Daniel's wisdom and the superiority of the God of Israel over pagan idols.

Additions to Daniel:
- Summary: Additions to Daniel includes both Susanna and Bel and the Dragon, along with additional material such as the Prayer of Azariah and the Song of the Three Holy Children.
- Difference: Whilst Susanna and Bel and the Dragon are included as separate stories in Additions to Daniel, they are not merged. Instead, they are presented alongside additional texts related to Daniel's experiences in Babylon.
- Content: Additions to Daniel includes material not found in Susanna and Bel and the Dragon, such as the Prayer of Azariah and the Song of the Three Holy Children. These additional texts contribute to the overall theme of faithfulness in the face of adversity and divine deliverance.

In summary, whilst Susanna and Bel and the Dragon are included in Additions to Daniel, they are not merged into a single narrative. Additions to Daniel contains additional content beyond these two stories, including the Prayer of Azariah and the Song of the Three Holy Children, which enrich the themes of faith, justice, and divine intervention found throughout the book of Daniel and its apocryphal additions.

Concision is the quality of giving only the information that is necessary and important, using few words.
Concision is expressing what needs to be said without using unnecessary words.
To be concise is to be brief but comprehensive.

Do you get get saved and kept, or do you get thrown into the fire? Keep what is of value, and bin, throw away, what is not.
Keep the ones who are of value, and bin, throw away, the ones who are not. It takes a beating to sort the one from the other.
Going through a hard and difficult life will make you better, or bitter; it will strengthen you and your faith and belief unto salvation, or not.
Are you going to let this life make you better, or bitter? Are you going to heaven or hell? You make the decision.
