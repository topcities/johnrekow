Commentary:
There are several places in the Bible, and in the traditions of cultural history, about the firstborn.
Referring to this general traditional history of ancient cultures, and to the content found throughout the Bible...
The firstborn, especially the firstborn son...
was given certain unique rights, responsibilities, and privileges,
was given priority and pre-eminence in the family, and the best of the inheritance,
was believed to represent the prime of human strength and vitality, from when the parents were young and healthy.
The firstborn was the "opener of the womb," and
became the primary heir of the family.
The firstborn's birthright traditionally involved a double portion of the inheritance of the household estate
and the firstborn was given the leadership of the family if the father became incapacitated or was absent for some reason.

In heaven, all of the saved, righteous children of God (the children of ELL-Low-Heem) will all be treated as if each one of them was the firstborn.
The saved believers will all be saved, forgiven, pure, clean, perfect, immortal, and righteous, and He will treat them all as if they were the firstborn.
Every child will be treated like the firstborn child in the kingdom of Heaven.

Hebrews 10:24-25
KJV: And let us consider one another to provoke unto love and to good works: Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as the manner of some is; but exhorting one another: and so much the more, as ye see the day approaching.
NLT: Let us think of ways to motivate one another to acts of love and good works. And let us not neglect our meeting together, as some people do, but encourage one another, especially now that the day of his return is drawing near.

Commentary:
When it comes to the the assembling of ourselves together, our meeting together, there are certain days that we are required by a permanent law to gather together in an official, set-apart, holy assembly.


In the NLT, there is what is called an official day for holy assembly.
In the KJV, the same thing is called a holy convocation.

What is identified as a permanent law in the NLT is called, in the KJV, an ordinance to thee and to thy sons for ever.
The KJV also talks about a permanent law by saying, that shall ye observe this day in your generations by an ordinance for ever.
So, the KJV ordinance for ever is called a permanent law in the NLT.
The KJV may also say that a permanent law shall be a statute for ever in your generations, throughout your generations in all your dwellings.
Let take a look at some official days for holy assembly and some permanent laws that are to be obeyed forever, no matter where you live.
These permanent laws must be observed wherever you live, forever.
It does not matter where you live right now. Wherever you live right now, you are to obey these permanent laws forever, and you should not forsake the meeting together, the assembling of ourselves together, on every official day for holy assembly.


Sabbath day of complete rest -- permanent law in the Ten Commandments.
Every Saturday is an official day for holy assembly.
