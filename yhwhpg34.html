Commentary:
There are several places in the Bible, and in the traditions of cultural history, about the firstborn.
Referring to this general traditional history of ancient cultures, and to the content found throughout the Bible...
The firstborn, especially the firstborn son...
was given certain unique rights, responsibilities, and privileges,
was given priority and pre-eminence in the family, and the best of the inheritance,
was believed to represent the prime of human strength and vitality, from when the parents were young and healthy.
The firstborn was the "opener of the womb," and
became the primary heir of the family.
The firstborn's birthright traditionally involved a double portion of the inheritance of the household estate
and the firstborn was given the leadership of the family if the father became incapacitated or was absent for some reason.

In heaven, all of the saved, righteous children of God (the children of ELL-Low-Heem) will all be treated as if each one of them was the firstborn.
The saved believers will all be saved, forgiven, pure, clean, perfect, immortal, and righteous, and He will treat them all as if they were the firstborn.
Every child will be treated like the firstborn child in the kingdom of Heaven.

Commentary: I could easily desire to share with you at least twenty more songs that I really enjoy from Phil Wickham.
In a moment, we will take a quick look at one that came out, back in the year of 2007.

This song really captures how I was feeling at many times during the year of 2023, right on up to and into November of 2023.
I would rather see myself and my family all depart from the earth in one moment, in the twinkling of an eye,
and see us all be, what one might call, being raptured or translated, like Enoch and Elijah did. I would love and prefer to see my family all depart at one moment like that, together, so that one would not have to mourn during the absence of the other.

But, if it should not be that way, and there was to be a funeral for me, I think that this next song is definitely the song I would want to have played at my funeral, or at the funeral of a believer that was suffering through hardships in their final days and months before they moved to their eternal, better dwelling place.

My only disappointment in this next song is the technical detail about the mentioning of tears of joy. There is no crying and no tears in heaven. Tears from earth are stored in bottles, and every tear is documented with the full story about why each and every tear was cried. This is what the Bible teaches, and we have studied this already, during a random Bible study moment, back in Christian Essays 28, also known as, The Bible's Greatest Hits, Chapter 28.

So, if you don't mind overlooking this small technical inaccuracy, the rest of this next song really tells the story behind how my heart was feeling at times, back in 2023, up into November 2023.
