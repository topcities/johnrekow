MidWest City Distances


This MidWest News Page covers the areas surrounding the cities mentioned here. Starting at the Canadian border in the north, we have the state of Minnesota. South of there is Iowa, with one of its largest cities being Des Moines. East of Iowa is Illinois, and the city of Chicago, Illinois, which is the third largest city in the United States, near the south end of Lake Michigan. Next, going South-West we find the state of Missouri. It has two big cities, Kansas City on the west border, and Saint Louis on the east border. Near the South East corner of Missouri is its third largest city, Springfield, Missouri. There are 33 other cities with that same name of Springfield in 24 other United States.

West of Missouri is Kansas, with its main city being, Wichita, Kansas.
South of Kansas is Oklahoma, with its main, large city being, Oklahoma City, Oklahoma.
East of Oklahoma is Arkansas. Arkansas is also the state directly south of Missouri.

Touching the North East corner or Arkansas is Tennessee, with Memphis, Tennessee being right near the Arkansas border.

Finally, from Arkansas we have Texas on the South West, Louisiana on the south, and Mississippi on the South East, and south of those states is a large sea known as the Gulf of Mexico.

Distances between some of these major cities...

Chicago, Illinois going south east to Saint Louis, Missouri, is 297 miles.
In Missouri, the distance between Kansas City on the west border, and Saint Louis on the east border is 248 miles.
Springfield, Missouri is 166 miles away from Kansas City in the north,
and Springfield is 217 miles away from Saint Louis in the north east.

The distance from Chicago, Illinois, to Dallas, Texas, is
926 miles with tolls, or 968 miles without tolls.


Here are distance information facts about some cities in the north-western corner of the state of Arkansas, known as the Fayetteville-Springdale-Rogers Metropolitan Area.

The following summary is based on time and distance calculations from Google Maps, and from the following webpage from Wikipedia, the free encyclopaedia...
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northwest_Arkansas

Northwest Arkansas (NWA) is a metropolitan area and region in Arkansas within the Ozark Mountains.
It includes four of the ten largest cities in Arkansas:
Fayetteville, Springdale, Rogers, and Bentonville.

These adjacent towns are in Benton and Washington counties, and the adjacent rural Madison County, Arkansas.
The United States Census Bureau has the area defined as the Fayetteville-Springdale-Rogers Metropolitan Statistical Area.

Springfield, Missouri to Fayetteville, Arkansas is 119 miles if travelling South West.
The distance is actually 136 mikes if going south first, through Branson.
Either route takes about the same amount of time, about 2 hours and 25 minutes, from Springfield, Missouri to Fayetteville, Arkansas.
