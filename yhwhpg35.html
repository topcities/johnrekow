"And Azazel taught men to make swords and knives and shields and breastplates, and made known to them the metals and the art of working them, and bracelets, and ornaments, and the use of rouge, and the beautifying of the eyelids, and all kinds of precious stones, and all colouring tinctures. And there was great impiety; they turned away from God and committed fornication; they were led astray and became corrupt in all their ways."


Commentary:
The term "antimony" in the R.H. Charles translation is translated as "rouge" in the Dead Sea Scrolls translation. Both terms refer to cosmetics, but "rouge" is a more specific modern term for a type of cosmetic.

The Dead Sea Scrolls translation uses "impiety" instead of "godlessness," which is a slight variation in word choice but conveys a similar meaning.

The phrase "there arose much godlessness" (Charles)
is rendered as
"there was great impiety" in the Dead Sea Scrolls translation.
Similarly, "they committed fornication, and they were led astray" (Charles)
is rendered as
"they turned away from God and committed fornication; they were led astray" in the Dead Sea Scrolls translation,
adding a specific mention of turning away from God.

These differences illustrate the subtle variations in translation choices and phrasing that can occur even when the overall meaning remains consistent. The more modern translation often aims for clarity and accessibility whilst maintaining fidelity to the original text.
