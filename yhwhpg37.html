Did Goliath have any brothers? Yes, he did. Goliath and his brothers can be found here:

2 Samuel 21:18-22 (NIV):
"In the course of time, there was a war with the Philistines at Gob. At that time, Sibbekai the Hushathite killed Saph, one of the descendants of the giants. In another battle with the Philistines at Gob, Elhanan son of Jaare-Oregim the Bethlehemite killed the brother of Goliath the Gittite, who had a spear with a shaft like a weaver's rod. In still another battle, which took place at Gath, there was a huge man with six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot—twenty-four in all. He also was descended from the giants. When he taunted Israel, Jonathan son of Shimeah, David's brother, killed him. These four were descendants of Rapha in Gath, and they fell at the hands of David and his men."

1 Chronicles 20:4-8 (NIV):
"In the course of the war against the Philistines, Elhanan son of Jair killed Lahmi the brother of Goliath, who was from Gath. The shaft of his spear was like a weaver's rod. In still another battle, which took place at Gath, there was a huge man with six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot—twenty-four in all. He too was descended from Rapha. When he taunted Israel, Jonathan son of Shimeah, David's brother, killed him. These were the descendants of Rapha in Gath, and they fell at the hands of David and his men."


Commentary:
These passages indicate that Goliath had brothers who were also giants, specifically mentioning that Lahmi was a brother of Goliath. The term "descendants of Rapha" refers to the giants, which implies that Goliath and his brothers were part of this lineage.


The identification of the individuals mentioned in the passages as brothers of Goliath is based on the context and wording used in the biblical texts. Here's how the connections are made:

Contextual Clues:
In both 2 Samuel 21:18-22 and 1 Chronicles 20:4-8, the narrative discusses battles involving the Philistines and mentions Goliath specifically. The passages refer to other giants who are described as being from Gath, the same city as Goliath, and they are noted as descendants of Rapha, which is a term often associated with giants.

Direct Mention:
In 1 Chronicles 20:4, it explicitly states that Elhanan killed "Lahmi the brother of Goliath." This direct mention establishes a familial relationship.

Similar Descriptions:
The other giants mentioned in these passages are described in ways that align with the characteristics of Goliath, such as their size and lineage. This suggests they are part of the same family or group of giants.

Repetition of Themes:
The mention of multiple giants in the context of battles against Israel, along with the specific reference to Goliath's brother, reinforces the idea that these individuals are related.

Whilst the texts do not provide a detailed family tree, the combination of these contextual clues and direct references leads to the conclusion that the giants mentioned in these passages are indeed brothers or close relatives of Goliath.


Based on these biblical accounts, here is a list of Goliath and his four brothers:

1. Goliath - The most well-known giant, defeated by David.
2. Lahmi - Specifically mentioned as the brother of Goliath in 1 Chronicles 20:4.
3. Saph - Mentioned in 2 Samuel 21:18 as one of the giants killed by David's men.
4. Ishbi-Benob - Also mentioned in 2 Samuel 21:16 as a giant who sought to kill David.
5. Another unnamed giant - Described in 2 Samuel 21:20 as a giant with six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot, who was also a descendant of the giants.

These names and descriptions are derived from the passages in 2 Samuel and 1 Chronicles, which discuss the battles with the Philistines and the giants associated with Goliath.


In the King James Version (KJV) of the Bible, the verse that describes David selecting smooth stones before fighting Goliath is found in 1 Samuel 17:40. Here is the verse:

1 Samuel 17:40 (KJV):
("And he took his staff in his hand, and chose him five smooth stones out of the valley, and put them in a shepherd's vessell which he had,)
"And he took his staff in his hand, and chose him five smooth stones out of the brook, and put them in a shepherd's bag which he had, even in a scrip; and his sling was in his hand: and he drew near to the Philistine."

Commentary:
Perhaps David chose five smooth stones before facing Goliath, because Goliath had four brothers, which would be a total of five giants known to be in the region.

The reason David chose five smooth stones before facing Goliath is not explicitly stated in the biblical text, so interpretations vary. Some scholars and commentators suggest that David may have selected five stones in anticipation of facing not only Goliath but also his brothers, as there are references to multiple giants associated with Goliath.

Ultimately, David only needed one stone to defeat Goliath, which emphasises his faith in God rather than reliance on physical weapons.

We only need one stone to defeat our enemy, and that stone is our chief cornerstone, the head stone of the corner, our firm foundation, the rock that travelled with them, standing in the shadow of a great rock in a weary land, the solid rock on which I stand. Who set me up upon a rock?
