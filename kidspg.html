American Girl Doll Skin Cleaning


American Girl Doll Skin Cleaning


Cleaning Your AG Doll's Skin

Information taken from American Girl website, and expounded on.

This method also works on other 18-inch dolls too.

Please note: Children should have help with this project. Never submerge your doll in water! American Girl dolls should not be given baths with real water. Their eyes can rust if they get wet, and the stuffing inside the doll's body can mould, and or disintegrate. Not liable for any damage or loss to you, or the doll, So, do this at your own risk! It is important to note that household cleaners, and vinyl cleaners can take colour out of the doll's "skin".

Step 1. Place 2 US tbsp (30 mL) of baking soda and 1 US tbsp (15 mL) of water into a small bowl. Make a cleansing paste by using a measuring spoon to add baking soda and water to a small bowl. Stir the water into the baking soda until the powder is all absorbed into the paste. The best consistency is that of toothpaste.
If the paste is too thick, add a few more drops of water. If it is very runny, add another pinch of baking soda to thicken it.

Step 2. Apply some baking soda paste to a warm, wet washcloth. Take a washcloth and wet it using warm water from the sink. Put a small amount onto a section of the washcloth. you can leave on the paste for 5 minutes at a time then cleaned off.

Step 3. Rub the baking soda paste in a circular motion into your doll's skin. Take the section of washcloth with paste on it and press it firmly into your doll's hard vinyl skin where the mark is on its face, arms, or legs. Rub the skin in a firm, circular motion with the washcloth until you see the mark fade. Repeat until the mark is gone.
This process fixes most paint, makeup, dirt, and marker stains.

Step 4. Wipe the residue away with a warm, wet washcloth. Wash the washcloth frequently as you wipe the doll. When the doll's skin is clean, rinse your washcloth under warm running water until all the baking soda paste is rinsed away. Use the washcloth to wipe any leftover baking soda paste off of the doll.

Step 5. Allow your doll to dry using a clean towel or have her air-dry. And you're done!

American Girl Doll Hair Fixing



American Girl Doll Hair Fixing


HOW TO FIX AMERICAN GIRL DOLL- HAIR

American Girl (AG) dolls are expensive, and I no longer recommend buying from the company, due to them showing their support for agendas starting back in 2015, in their magazine, catalogue, clothes, and even special dolls... Things little girls should not have to think about. And a toy company should not be putting political opinions into a toy. They also have done a collaboration with Harry potter (witchcraft).

If you want to get a brand-new doll of excellence, Gotz dolls are maybe better then AG!

SHOP:  Gotz (Federal Republic of Germany) -- Doll and Puppet Manufacturer:  English Page for Gotz High-Quality Dolls (Some dolls may have explicit anatomy)
https://www.goetz-puppen.de/en/

Plus Gotz dolls are made in Germany, the hair is way better, I had her 5+ years before I got an AG doll I used a regular hair brush, and her hair is better then the newer AG dolls I own.

A LITTLE DISCLAIMER AND REMINDER:
Please note: These methods do NOT make the doll's hair like new because, if you've had a new doll, you know how that feels. But it does do an amazing job. And this can breathe new life into a treasured doll. I am not liable for any damage or loss to you, or your doll. These are my findings, and what others have confirmed to work in our experience, and does not mean you will have the same result, do at your own risk!

First: Keeping AG doll hair nice
AG doll's hair is made from wig grade artificial hair a blend of mod-acrylic fibres. It's designed to be used by people that want or need to wear wigs. With that being said, the wigs are good quality designed to last long time, even designed to withstand low heat for styling (Though never marketed on AG's website to do so)the hair can withstand 100s of styles, and the hair even washable. But not the scalp/root.

There are two hair tools the AG makes, a deep maroon coloured wooden brush, with metal bristles, and a violet coloured wire pick. Both have American Girl marked on them.

For wavy hair or straight hair use a wire wig brush. For curly hair or textured hair use the hair pick.

Boar-bristle brushes work really good, boar bristles are softer, and more flexible.

I did not have an AG doll brush for years. I used a boar bristles brush and it worked great, I even noticed some breakage from the hair when I finally got the AG brush. So I prefer a Bore bristles brush for any hair, real, or fake.

The American Girl doll hospital can fix anything...anything. But the price can be as much or more then a new doll. And it takes weeks to get sent, repaired, and delivered back to you. So it is good to know how to fix problems on your own. But it is nice to know that is always an option if all else fails.

I would not recommend using a hairdryer, it is not human hair and you run the risk of melting or warping the hair or the hard plastic of the doll.


STYLING, AND GENERAL HAIR USE

AG's website says to hold the doll's head firmly and use a spray bottle of water or a misting bottle to lightly mist the hair with just enough water to make it easier to work with. Beware don't ever get water into the eyes, they can mould, rust, and lose their blinking ability. Never use any styling products, ie sprays, creams, etc. Cool, or room temperature is best, due to the hotter temperatures removing any style (i.e. wave, curl, or kinky curl). But warm or hot can be used to re-style a dolls hair. In a blog I found a former AG employee reached out and recommended using cold or lukewarm water as hot water can remove the curl/wave of the hair. That is also my findings.


BRUSHING:
Take out any clips or hair ties in her hair, and any accessories from the dolls hair before you start styling.
1. Firmly hold the doll by sitting her between your legs, then grasp the neck, but not so hard as to loosen the head. If you don't have a good hold on your doll, you might accidentally yank too hard and loosen her head.
2. Lightly mist the hair, just enough water to make it easier to work with. (Don't get any water on or in the eyes. Water can be on the face)
3. Take hold of a small section of hair near the ends, then gently brush or pick out the tangles. If there are any hard tangles, use your fingers to untangle the hair strand by strand.
4. Keep working with small sections, moving up toward the wig's base, starting at the end of the hair working your way up, but always brushing or picking down toward the ends. And keeping the hair a little damp, helps the hair not get fizzy.

STYLING: Waves and Curls
Only ever use fabric bands, or scrunchies to style, the rubber bands cause breakage. Claw clips are ok too, and also clips. Doll companies, including AG, make doll size curlers too.

Waves: make sure the hair is damp. To create easy waves by braiding your doll's hair while it's wet. Creating 1 or 2 big braids, or lots of little ones, or my favourite is to do a two section twist braid, is an easy way to make your doll's hair wavy. Like for the doll "Kanoni". While her hair is still wet, braid it back tightly into however many braids you want. Leave the braids in for a day or so, then take them out for a permanent wave style.
The longer you leave the braids in, the longer the waves will last.

For the tightest kinky curls, wind the strand of hair around a pen or unsharpened pencil. Hold the hair around the pencil for a couple of seconds, then slowly slide the brush out. Or even use claw clips to keep the pencils/pens in place for however long you want.

You can also finger curl if your doll's hair is curly. After you've picked through your doll's curly hair, separate a small section. Smooth it out with your hand, then coil and twirl it around your finger to create a curl.

Make sure your doll's hair is damp when you curl it. You can spritz it with the spray bottle again if you need to.


SHAMPOOING AND CONDITIONING:
When your American Girl doll's hair gets dirty or messy, the first thing you want to do is make her locks smooth and shiny once again by washing them. American Girl recommends that you send your doll to one of their doll doctors if you need to wash her hair, but if you don't want to wait weeks, and spend a lot of money you can give this a try. Use gentle products (never dish-washing soaps) and be very careful not to get her body wet. If your doll has short hair, like Kit or Nellie, it's better not to wash it at all, because you could damage her scalp or skin. Try using just a damp cloth instead.

Only wash your doll's hair once every few years while she is in use. Try to wash your doll's hair as little as possible to avoid accidentally spilling water on her or creating product build-up in her strands. Only wash it when it's noticeably dirty.

Your regular doll hair care routine should be simple misting, and brushing.

Step 1. Cover your doll's face and body with plastic so she doesn't get wet. Wrapping a plastic grocery bag or plastic wrap (saran wrap) around your doll's soft body and face to keep the water off of her. Water can damage your doll's stuffing and eyes.
Pull the bag right up to her hairline.
For extra protection, close your doll's eyes before you cover her. Add a little bit of painters tape where needed.
If you don't have any of this to cover the doll, be very careful not to get any water on your doll's face or body.

Step 2. Make sure there is no items in, or on the hair.

Step 3. Lay your doll on a bathroom counter with her hair in the sink. Place your doll down on a bathroom or kitchen counter with her head over the edge of the sink. Lay her hair out in the sink.

Step 4. Rinse her hair under the water. Turn on the water a gentle stream and run your doll's hair under it, from the tips up to about 1 inch (2.5 cm) from her scalp. Try not to get her scalp wet, it can damage her wig and loosen the glue holding it in place. And don't have the water to high, you don't want to have the water splash.
The water should not be too hot or too cold. It should be about room temperature, or a little bit warm.

Step 5. Wash her hair with a bit of mild shampoo, then rinse it out. There are special synthetic wig shampoo, but mild shampoo, or baby shampoo works good Squeeze a dime-sized amount of shampoo into your palm and scrunch it up through your doll's hair, just like you would with your hair. Rinse her hair out under the sink when you're done lathering.
Stop shampooing about 1 inch (2.5 cm) from her scalp so you don't get her head wet.
If your doll has anything stuck in her hair, like glue or gum, work those strands with your fingers until you can feel it loosening. It's OK if it doesn't all come out now -- you'll be brushing it out later.

Step 6. Rub a little bit of conditioner through her hair for about 5 minutes, then rinse it. Pour a dime-sized amount of conditioner into your palm. Massage it into your doll's hair, starting at the ends and working up to about 1 inch (2.5 cm) from her head.
If your doll has curly hair, the conditioner might make the curls a little less tight.
Conditioning your doll's hair is optional, but it can help to make your doll's hair less frizzy. You can use any kind of conditioner you want.

Step 7. Pat her hair with a towel until it's only a little damp. Lay your doll's head on a towel and spread out her hair above her head. Get a another one and put it on top of the hair and pat until the hair is only a little damp.
You want to brush her hair while it's still damp because brushing it when it's already dry can make it frizz. See the section above: BRUSHING


DIFFERENT METHODS TO FIX AMERICAN GIRL DOLL HAIR

A LITTLE DISCLAIMER AND REMINDER:
Please note: These methods will NOT make the doll's hair like new again, because, if you've had a new doll, you know how that feels. But these suggestions will do an amazing job. Doing these things can breathe new life into a treasured doll. I am not liable for any damage or loss to you, or your doll. These suggestions are my findings, and what others have confirmed to work, in our experience, and does not mean that you will have the same result.

PREP THE HAIR BY WASHING.
Make sure you have an American Girl Doll brush, or boar bristles brush before starting this process. You will want to start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up the hair, brushing the hair really well before straightening.

Cleaning the hair:
It is good to clean the hair before you straighten. you don't know how dirty the hair gets, you may see the hair become it's true lighter hue.

METHOD 1: STRAIGHTENING IRON
Pre Prep the hair, by SHAMPOOING AND CONDITIONING

Step 1. Get a hair straightener and put it on the lowest setting it can be on.

Step 2. Spot test on a small bit of hair from the lower part of the wig, on an end. (Not all straighteners heat the same, which is why it is important you try at the back of the head under a piece of the hair. I would not try this for just a small amount of frizzy doll hair. (Proceed at your own risk!) Remember to keep moving the iron down the hair, or the hair will melt!

Step 3. On the lowest setting of the iron, take a small section of hair (it is easier to slide the iron down the hair this way) and start near the scalp, never toughening the head with the iron, and go down Repeating as needed. Once that section is to your liking, repeat dozens of more times until the hair is done.

Note: If it is hard for you to move the iron down the hair, you can also use a spray bottle to lightly dampen the hair with water before straightening. It is not a requirement, but it can help make the process easier. Do not turn up the temperature, as you don't want to melt the hair. You will just need to be careful and patient. You can't undo it if you melt the hair!
