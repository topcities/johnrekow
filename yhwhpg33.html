They Love His Holy Name

The End of the Book of Enoch is a very interesting area to study.
This is Enoch's last chance to tell the people what he has seen just before is ascends up into heaven.
Jasher Chapter 3 tells us that a fiery, horse-like creature is pacing back and forth up in the sky, waiting for Enoch to finish up his message before transporting him to heaven.
Let's read these last few verses of The End of the Book of Enoch here next.
As you carefully study this, you will see how he sees a hell-like place where sinners go,
and he sees the kingdom of Heaven, where the Saints, the Elect Ones, the holy, set-apart believers, are going.
He looks back and forth, between these two eternal destinations.
Notice carefully how he describes the Saints, the Elect Ones, the holy, set-apart believers.
He describes the how their life has been, and all of the bad things that happened to them, just before they go to heaven.
The believers realise how short and temporary their earthly life is, and the many bad ways how the world has treated them.
They love and prefer the eternal life that is to come more than their temporary life in this physical world.
In spite of all the abuse, they remain clean and pure, and they love His Holy Name, as He leads them home and gives them rest.
You will see all of this and so much more, just in these last few verses of the Book of Enoch. Check it out...

Abraham and Sarah are generally a model couple, but their impatience, when it came to having a child, shows us how rushing a process can lead to stress.

Although God had blessed Abraham and promised to make him a great nation, all that seemed insignificant to Abraham in light of him not having any children.

As they got older, it seemed more unlikely that they would have a child of their own. So in their impatience, they concluded that it was probably through another woman that they would have the promised child. As a result, Abraham slept with Sarah's servant Hagar.

Hagar conceived, but when the child was born, it was the start of stress for both Abraham and Sarah. Hagar began to despise Sarah and in turn, Sarah began to cast blame on Abraham for her misery.

In time, Abraham did have a child through Sarah as God intended, but since they were impatient, they had brought on themselves stress on the path to getting there.

Although God chose the Israelites to be a blessing to other nations, they had much to work on as individuals. One of those things was patience. In one particular story, their impatience caused them to do something so bad in the sight of God that it made God want to destroy them.

In the eyes of the Israelites, Moses was taking too long to come down from the mountain and so they told Moses' assistant Aaron:

"Come, make us gods who will go before us. As for this fellow Moses who brought us up out of Egypt, we don't know what has happened to him."
(Exodus 32:1 NIV)


Sensing their impatience, Aaron gave in to their request and used their gold earrings to make them an idol in the form of a golden calf.

That made God angry and He was prepared to destroy them. Thankfully for them, Moses convinced God to relent from his anger, but in the end, they were still punished. About 3,000 of them died because of actions stemming from impatience (Genesis 32:28).

A lesson that stands out in the story of the golden calf is that our impatience could cause us to seek inferior substitutes. In the case of the Israelites, Moses' delay led them to find an idol to worship instead.
