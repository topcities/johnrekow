To repent means to change direction. How do we repent?

Romans 2:4 (KJV)
...the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance.

Strong's Greek Definition for Goodness comes from the Greek word "chrestos"
which means, kind, easy, better, gracious, fit for use, useful, virtuous, good, benevolent, manageable, mild, pleasant (as opposed to being harsh, hard, sharp or bitter)

The word, "benevolent" is an interesting word.

The Chambers 21st Century Dictionary says that benevolent is "showing or involving kindness and generosity... for the benefit of others rather than for profit; charitable."


Romans 2:4 (NLT)
...Can't you see that his kindness is intended to turn you from your sin?

Romans 2:4 (NIV)
...do you show contempt for the riches of his kindness, forbearance and patience, not realising that God's kindness is intended to lead you to repentance?

A form of the word, "benevolent" is something we will see again, here, shortly, in the Eth Cepher, in the Book of Enoch.

Commentary: As we now finish this look at these selected passages from the Book of Enoch, here is one final thought to ponder.
In that last verse, we see...
the souls of those who utter complaints,
and of those who watch for their destruction.
Those who watch for their destruction?
Think about that. We are told to to be sober and watch.
Being a sober watchman is one thing, but maybe, this verse may suggest that being extra worried and fearful of coming destruction can develop into a form of sin.
The Bible does promise that, the fearful shall go into the lake which burneth with fire.
Let us be a vigilant, sober watchmen, without becoming fearful of coming destruction, and may we not have a certain fearful looking for of judgement.
