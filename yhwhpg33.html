They Love His Holy Name

The End of the Book of Enoch is a very interesting area to study.
This is Enoch's last chance to tell the people what he has seen just before is ascends up into heaven.
Jasher Chapter 3 tells us that a fiery, horse-like creature is pacing back and forth up in the sky, waiting for Enoch to finish up his message before transporting him to heaven.
Let's read these last few verses of The End of the Book of Enoch here next.
As you carefully study this, you will see how he sees a hell-like place where sinners go,
and he sees the kingdom of Heaven, where the Saints, the Elect Ones, the holy, set-apart believers, are going.
He looks back and forth, between these two eternal destinations.
Notice carefully how he describes the Saints, the Elect Ones, the holy, set-apart believers.
He describes the how their life has been, and all of the bad things that happened to them, just before they go to heaven.
The believers realise how short and temporary their earthly life is, and the many bad ways how the world has treated them.
They love and prefer the eternal life that is to come more than their temporary life in this physical world.
In spite of all the abuse, they remain clean and pure, and they love His Holy Name, as He leads them home and gives them rest.
You will see all of this and so much more, just in these last few verses of the Book of Enoch. Check it out...

The Torah Before Torah

When we did the Bible study, titled, The Hired and The Prodigal,
which was back in Christian Essays 6,
we had mentioned this little bit of passage from Genesis 19.

Genesis 19: 5-7 -- KJV
19:5 And they called unto Lot, and said unto him, Where are the men which came in to thee this night? bring them out unto us, that we may know them.
19:6 And Lot went out at the door unto them, and shut the door after him,
19:7 And said, I pray you, brethren, do not so wickedly.


Genesis 19: 5-7 -- NLT
5 They shouted to Lot, "Where are the men who came to spend the night with you? Bring them out to us so we can have sex with them!"
6 So Lot stepped outside to talk to them, shutting the door behind him.
7 "Please, my brothers," he begged, "don't do such a wicked thing.

Lot is saying, "don't do such a wicked thing."
Moses had not yet been born, and the Torah, the commandments in the Bible,
had not yet been given out. How did Lot know that it was a wicked thing?
Was there some kind of set of laws, some set of instructions and commandments, some kind of Torah before there was a Torah?
Yes. Was it written down? Maybe, or it was at least taught orally, from generation to generation, until Moses wrote it all down again.
One indication that there was a Torah before there offically was a writen-down Torah, is found in The Book of Jasher.
Can you guess who it was that originally presented this first version of a Torah?
Let's find out!


The Book of Jasher is not in the Bible anymore.
This English translation is from a Hebrew book printed in 1613.
The paper version was printed and published in Salt Lake City,
by J.H. Parry and Company, in the year 1887.
