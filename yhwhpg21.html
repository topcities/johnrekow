There is a traditional concept of when two parties would cut a covenant.
This is the ancient, traditional way how two people would make a promise to each other, or an agreement, or a contract.
They would take an animal, like, let's say for example, a calf.
They butcher it into two halves, and then make a little walkway between the two halves.
Next, they walk between the halves, from one end to the other, and exchange the terms of the contract or agreement or promise.
What this means is, if you don't keep the terms of this contract, may you then be cut up into pieces like this animal right here.
Likewise, if I don't keep the terms of this contract, may you then cut me up into pieces like this animal right here.
Then, after both agree to this covenant, they sign the contract and make it an officially done deal, by roasting the animal in fire,
then they basically have a sort of big barbeque, celebrating the contract as being done, signed, and agreed upon.

Dear Lord Jesus, Dear Yaw, ELL-Low-Heem, Yaw-Who-Wah, Yaw-Who-Shaw,
From a personal and rather selfish perspective, I want to walk down the right path that leads to Better, for myself, personally, and for my family.
