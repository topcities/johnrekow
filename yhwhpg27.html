She says to me with a big smile, when I grow up I want to be a doctor!

And so I point to an area at the top of my arm, next to my head, and I ask her, what is this called? She says, that is your shoulder. And then I say to her, that is wrong! You are incorrect, and I am very offended that you call that a shoulder! This exact spot right here is called, the clavicle, and that is the medically correct term for this particular area that you call a shoulder. How can you call yourself a doctor when you don't even know the how to pronounce the correct names? And so, she runs away, crying, and decides to never be a doctor because of how mean and cruel I was to her, even though I was technically correct in calling that the clavicle and not a shoulder. We need to be sensitive and supportive to those that don't know what we know at this point of their life, and we need to encourage people to keep learning and growing in their knowledge of the person that they want to become.

Are you like that with people? Are you mean and cruel to those that don't know what you know? You're not? Are you sure? Let me tell the story again, and I will change a few things, and let's see what happens here.

Let's say that, I go to a church, and I happen to see a 5-year-old girl pretending to read a children's Bible. She has a neat Bible with pictures in it, and she has next to her some pictures that she coloured that illustrate stories in the Bible, and her Bible bookmark has a Christian cross on it with John 3:16 quoted on it. She says to me with a big smile, when I die, I'm going to live forever in the kingdom of heaven! Jesus is the king and he lives in my heart!

And so I point to her bookmark, and I read part of John 3:16, For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son. I ask her, what is the name of the only begotten Son? She says, Jesus. And then I say to her, that is wrong! You are incorrect, and I am very offended that you call the only begotten Son Jesus! His correct name is pronounced Yaw-Who-Shaw. How can you call yourself a saved, believing Christian going to heaven when you don't even know the how to pronounce the correct names? And so, she runs away, crying, and decides to never read her Bible or go to church ever again because of how mean and cruel I was to her, even though I was technically correct in calling the only begotten Son Yaw-Who-Shaw and not Jesus. We need to be sensitive and supportive to those that don't know what we know at this point of their life, and we need to encourage people to keep learning and growing in their knowledge of the Bible and in their development and strengthening of their marriage relationship with the Heavenly Father, and his only begotten Son and in the Spirit that is Holy, his Sevenfold Spirit, so that people can walk even more deeply, strongly and intimately in the way they need to go, in the truth that they need to believe and receive, and in the eternal, everlasting life that they need in order to live, forever.

To them, Hebrew is an unknown tongue, an unknown language, and the Bible warns us not to speak in unknown languages without any interpretation. I'm thankful that we can know you in a deeper and more intimate level by calling on your actual, true Hebrew name, and that you have revealed yourself to us in a clearer and more in-depth way, by showing us more of the truth about what has happened to your word as it was copied and translated throughout the centuries of time, and we love how you are showing us again additional books that were taken away from us in previous centuries, but now, we need your help in loving others around us who don't know you very well. They may have an obvious amount of sin and lawlessness, and we see that sin all around us, but you say to us, I'm holding you to a higher standard because you are actually studying Torah and they are not, so since you know and study Torah, I expect you to follow Torah and obey it, even if everyone else around you does not.

When we detect these technical mistakes, these technicalities, sometimes we stop at the technical mistake and we do not receive the message, the meaning and the teaching of the communication. I mean, did you look beyond the immediate, and pull out the message that was behind all of those technical mistakes? Your house is on fire, get out now! Did you actually get the message?
