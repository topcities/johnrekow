Let's take a moment to read the lyrics to a few interesting Christian songs.

At the end of August 2022, my family was enjoying two new songs from TobyMac's latest album, which had just been released.

One of those songs featured TobyMac with his mates from DC Talk, basically performing what could be thought of as a brand new song from DC Talk.



TobyMac was originally a member of DC Talk from 1987 to 2001.
Since 2001, TobyMac has released eight studio albums, two Christmas albums, five remix albums, three live albums, and three extended plays as a solo artist.


His eighth studio album, Life After Death
was released on
Friday 19 August 2022
and is available as a digital download, a physical Audio CD, or as an analogue, vinyl long-playing record.


The title of this album, and some of the content in his recent songs may be influenced by the tragic death of his son.
TobyMac has five children, and it was his firstborn son,
Truett McKeehan, that was found dead at his home in Nashville on 23 October 2019, at age 21,
of what appears to be, or what has been said to be,
an accidental drug overdose of fentanyl and amphetamines.

One of the songs on the Life After Death album is titled, Faithfully.

Micah 7: 7-20 (NIV)
7
But as for me, I watch in hope for the Lord,
I wait for God my Saviour;
my God will hear me.
8
Do not gloat over me, my enemy!
Though I have fallen, I will rise.
Though I sit in darkness,
the Lord will be my light.
9
Because I have sinned against him,
I will bear the Lord's wrath,
until he pleads my case
and upholds my cause.
He will bring me out into the light;
I will see his righteousness.
10
Then my enemy will see it
and will be covered with shame,
she who said to me,
"Where is the Lord your God?"
My eyes will see her downfall;
even now she will be trampled underfoot
like mire in the streets.
11
The day for building your walls will come,
the day for extending your boundaries.
12
In that day people will come to you
from Assyria and the cities of Egypt,
even from Egypt to the Euphrates
and from sea to sea
and from mountain to mountain.
13
The earth will become desolate because of its inhabitants,
as the result of their deeds.
14
Shepherd your people with your staff,
the flock of your inheritance,
which lives by itself in a forest,
in fertile pasturelands.
(in the middle of Carmel)
Let them feed in Bashan and Gilead
as in days long ago.
15
"As in the days when you came out of Egypt,
I will show them my wonders."
16
Nations will see and be ashamed,
deprived of all their power.
They will put their hands over their mouths
and their ears will become deaf.
17
They will lick dust like a snake,
like creatures that crawl on the ground.
They will come trembling out of their dens;
they will turn in fear to the Lord our God
and will be afraid of you.
18
Who is a God like you,
who pardons sin and forgives the transgression
of the remnant of his inheritance?
You do not stay angry forever
but delight to show mercy.
19
You will again have compassion on us;
you will tread our sins underfoot
and hurl all our iniquities into the depths of the sea.
20
You will be faithful to Jacob,
and show love to Abraham,
as you pledged on oath to our ancestors
in days long ago.

Micah 7: 7-20 (NLT)
7
As for me, I look to the Lord for help.
I wait confidently for God to save me,
and my God will certainly hear me.
8
Do not gloat over me, my enemies!
For though I fall, I will rise again.
Though I sit in darkness,
the Lord will be my light.
9
I will be patient as the Lord punishes me,
for I have sinned against him.
But after that, he will take up my case
and give me justice for all I have suffered from my enemies.
The Lord will bring me into the light,
and I will see his righteousness.
10
Then my enemies will see that the Lord is on my side.
They will be ashamed that they taunted me, saying,
"So where is the Lord --
that God of yours?"
With my own eyes I will see their downfall;
they will be trampled like mud in the streets.
11
In that day, Israel, your cities will be rebuilt,
and your borders will be extended.
12
People from many lands will come and honour you --
from Assyria all the way to the towns of Egypt,
from Egypt all the way to (the river,) the Euphrates River,
and from distant seas and mountains.
13
But (the earth and) the land will become empty and desolate
because of the wickedness of those who live there.
14
O Lord, protect your people with your shepherd's staff;
lead your flock, your special possession.
Though they live alone in a thicket
(surrounded by a fruitful land)
on the heights of Mount Carmel,
let them graze in the fertile pastures of Bashan and Gilead
as they did long ago.
15
"Yes," says the Lord,
"I will do mighty miracles for you,
like those I did when I rescued you
from slavery in Egypt."
16
All the nations of the world will stand amazed
at what the Lord will do for you.
They will be embarrassed
at their feeble power.
They will cover their mouths in silent awe,
deaf to everything around them.
17
Like snakes crawling from their holes,
they will come out to meet the Lord our God.
They will fear him greatly,
trembling in terror at his presence.
18
Where is another God like you,
who pardons the guilt of the remnant,
overlooking the sins of his special people?
You will not stay angry with your people forever,
because you delight in showing unfailing love.
19
Once again you will have compassion on us.
You will trample our sins under your feet
and throw them into the depths of the ocean!
20
You will show us your faithfulness and unfailing love
as you promised to our ancestors Abraham and Jacob long ago.
